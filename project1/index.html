Project 1

Introduction

This project showcases a collection of historical photographs that have been processed and colorized using advanced image processing techniques. Each section displays a comparison between the original white cropped version and the colorized result, demonstrating the transformation of black and white imagery into vibrant, lifelike representations.

The general approach taken to solve this task is to crop out the outer white border automatically, and then finetune by applying extra crop when necessary. The colorization is done by splitting the image into three channels, and then aligning by comparing the red channel and green channel to the blue channel. Each alignment is done by using normalized cross-correlation to calculate the minimum difference between the two channels. This is based on the assumption that the channels share brightness, so where the image is bright, all channels should be bright, giving us a correlation to work with. After aligning, we stack all channels on top of each other, and we get the colorized image as shown below.

Below, the left image is the cropped image used to generate the image on the right. You can see that for most of the images, the white border, and most of the black borders, has been cropped. This resulted in more stable and accurate colorization results. The auto-crop only tried to crop out the white borders, and stops when the entire column or row isn't all white. Then it requires manual tuning and cropping to further finetune the result.
